No fancy research methods were used

Sometimes is allabout getting your hands dirty

Problem

Lil Homes was a company that built modular tiny houses.

Their clients were third parties that decorated homes to match their buyers needs.

One day a client called saying that they wanted to stop buying as soon as possible.

He complained that the homes were not small enough and that could impact his manufacturing.

The brand new management team had barely any context on the subject.

Process

First of all, I read their guidelines to try to understand what were they referring to as “not small enough”.

We need concrete evidence of what our products were doing wrong.

After our first analysis, everything seemed up to standards. The homes were in fact built within the authorized dimensions.

To understand why they got that impression I searched the web for our product branding.

Quickly I found out a lot of misleading and obsolete advertisements on each of the Lil Home’s microsites.

Fig.1 - A picture of the website content analysis

I took notes of all the key points in our microsites.

I identified the owners of each microsite in our company, in case we’d have to delegate some tasks and needed to act fast.

I asked around trying to understand the motivations behind the advertisement.

After one day of talking to people from development to marketing, I concluded that the problem was a branding issue.

With sufficient context gathered I defined a two-phased action plan:

Fig.2 - A picture of the action plan

A short term solution that allowed us to act fast if the client would stop buying from Lil Homes.

A medium-term solution to avoid such a misunderstanding happening again.

Outcome

At the end the advertisement and branding image were updated.

We never got any answer from our client and they did not break contract with us.

Learnings

When time is short we do not have the luxury to prepare a proper research method.

Sometimes we need to acknowledge that any type of research is still research.

What’s most important is to know what to do and when to do it.
